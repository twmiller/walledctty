Hardware

WalledCTTY is powered by a 40 MHz AMD 80386 processor, which was the fastest 386 available at the time of its release, capable of handling complex tasks and multitasking processes. The Shuttle HOT-307 motherboard, with its 512k cache, is also a relatively high-performance component from its time. It's maxed out at 32 MB of memory which would have been a substantial amount of memory in its day. Two 500 GB hard drives offer ample storage space, both for the BBS and related software, and also for backups. The System also includes an ATI Mach 8 video card which was a decent graphics card when it was new, and offers excellent software support. Finally, a 3COM Etherlink III network card provides reliable connectivity to the wider network, not only allowing users to connect to the system via telnet, but also paving the way to allow the BBS to share some of its files with this website. Lastly, there's a 16550 serial port card that's connected to a U.S. Robotics v.92 56k external modem.

Software

The operating system running on the WalledCTTY BBS server is IBM's OS/2 Warp 3 Connect. This is the last version of OS/2 that will run on the 80386 family of processors, and includes a modern enough TCP/IP stack that we can achieve the connectivity we need to get into and out of the system. In conjunction with the TCP/IP stack, we're using Ray Gwinn's fantastic SIO comm drivers and virtual modems. The BBS software we're using is T.A.G. 2.7d2, which was the last release of the T.A.G. software. T.A.G. is a very powerful BBS software package, and is capable of handling a large number of users and a large number of messages. It's also very customizable, and we've made a number of changes to the software to make it more suitable for our purposes. We've also added a number of doors to the system, including many popular games from the early to mid 1990s.

Miscellaneous

  • The Telnet & Dial-Up BBS Guide is the largest regularly maintained Bulletin Board System (BBS) guide that lists both Telnet (Internet based) and Dial-Up (modem based) BBS systems.
  • fsxNet is a fun, simple and experimental (fidonet-type) network established in late 2015. "fsxNet is not a prescriptive network. e[X]perimentation is at heart of its name and members are actively encouraged to be creative in their use of the network. A limited number of message areas and file bases are used - setup is quick and [S]imple."
  • Break Into Chat - A wiki and blog about bulletin board systemm door games. "TradeWars 2002. Legend of the Red Dragon. Solar Realms Elite. Classic games that were played once upon a time on dialup bulletin board systems. The Break Into Chat wiki was created to preserve the history of these and other BBS door games, after editors on Wikipedia began deleting door game articles."
  • Agency BBS - "Agency BBS is a Bulletin Board System that first ran in Hamilton, New Zealand between 1993-1995. Out of nostalgia and curiousity it was resurrected in late 2012 and has become a source of enjoyment for its SysOp (system operator) Paul Hayton (aka Avon) and the many users from around the globe that login and use it for messaging, online games, etc." Avon, the SysOp, is the head honcho of FSXnet.
